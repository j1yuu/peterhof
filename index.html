What history stands by Peterhof?

In 1715 Peter the Great decided to create a front imperial residence that could surpass the famous Versailles.

Original plan was to start developing residence in Strelna, but due to the need to raise the water-level by 10 meters above the sea, that would`ve leaded to flooding near rivers and villages, engineers convinced Peter to replace parks in Peterhof.

So, the grand opening of Peterhof fountains was in the 1723 year.

After the revolution of 1917 all of the palaces were turned into the museums. During the WW2, especially during the Leningrad blockade, Peterhof was occupated by German devisions. Before their arrival, over 8,000 pieces of furniture and 50 statues were evacuated, but it wasn`t enough to rescue the whole palace-park ensamble. Restoration of ensamble started just after the ending of blockade. 155 museum objects were restorated by the 1985.

How to get to Peterhof?

Peterhof is located on the southwestern shore of the Gulf of Finland.

You can get there by minibus, bus or electrotrain
